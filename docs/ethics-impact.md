# Ethics Impact Assessment — Hospital / Clinic Management System

## Stakeholders

| Stakeholder | Role | Interest |
|-------------|------|----------|
| Patients | End users | Privacy and security of their medical records |
| Doctors | End users | Accurate and accessible patient information |
| Receptionists | End users | Easy-to-use appointment and billing system |
| Clinic Admin | Manager | Efficient operations and data reporting |
| Developers | Builders | Clean, maintainable, and secure codebase |
| Government/Regulators | Oversight | Compliance with health data laws |

## Potential Ethical Risks

| ID | Ethical Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|----|-------------|-----------|--------|------------|
| E-01 | Unauthorized access to patient medical records | High | Critical | Implement role-based access control and encryption |
| E-02 | Bias in doctor scheduling algorithm | Medium | High | Ensure fair scheduling without discrimination |
| E-03 | Data sold or shared without patient consent | Low | Critical | Strict data privacy policy and no third-party sharing |
| E-04 | System errors causing wrong prescriptions | Low | Critical | Require doctor confirmation before saving prescriptions |
| E-05 | Exclusion of elderly or disabled users | Medium | High | Design accessible UI following WCAG guidelines |

## Ethical Principles Applied
- **Transparency** — Patients are informed about what data is collected
- **Fairness** — All patients receive equal access to services
- **Accountability** — All actions are logged and traceable
- **Privacy** — Patient data is encrypted and access-controlled
- **Non-maleficence** — System is designed to prevent harm to patients
